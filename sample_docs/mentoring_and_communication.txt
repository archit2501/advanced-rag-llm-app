MENTORING, COMMUNICATION, AND ESCALATION
Programme Office | Summer 2026

Each intern has a primary faculty mentor and a day-to-day research mentor. The intern and at least one mentor hold a 30-minute one-to-one meeting every week. The intern sends a short agenda before the meeting and records decisions and action items in the approved project workspace.

Routine blockers should first be raised with the day-to-day mentor. If the mentor has not responded within two working days, or if the issue concerns that mentor, the intern should contact the faculty mentor or programme lead. Safety, harassment, ethics, and security concerns may be reported directly through the specialist channels without waiting two days. Retaliation for good-faith reporting is prohibited.

The project chat workspace may contain schedules, public papers, and ordinary technical discussion. It must not contain credentials, identifiable participant data, confidential sponsor material, or private personnel concerns. Assessment is based on the published rubric and evidence of work; no intern may be failed solely on one mentor's undocumented opinion.
